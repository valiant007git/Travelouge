Sitong

The Hidden Orange Village of North Bengal

About Sitong

Sitong, often referred to as the 'Orange Village', is a cluster of pristine hamlets situated in the Kurseong hills. Known for its lush orange orchards that turn the valley a vibrant hue during winter, Sitong is an offbeat paradise. It offers unhindered views of the Kanchenjunga, serene forests perfect for bird-watching, and a peaceful retreat away from commercialized tourist spots.

Best Time To Visit

November to January is the peak season to witness the orange orchards in full bloom. The weather is crisp and clear.

Top Attractions

Orange Orchards

Walk through the vibrant orchards in winter.

Ahaldara Viewpoint

Offers a stunning 360-degree view of Kanchenjunga and the Teesta river.

Namthing Pokhari

A seasonal lake known for the rare Himalayan Salamander.

Old Bamboo Monastery

A quaint, peaceful monastery made mostly of bamboo.

Local Food Highlights

  • Fresh Oranges: Plucked straight from the orchards.
  • Local Homestay Food: Organic, farm-to-table Nepali cuisine.
  • Local Tea: Enjoy a hot cup while taking in the views.

Sample Itinerary (2D/1N)

Day 1: Arrival in Sitong

Pick up from NJP/Bagdogra and transfer to Sitong. Check in to your homestay. After lunch, visit the local orange orchards and the Old Bamboo Monastery. Evening at leisure with a bonfire at your homestay. Overnight stay in Sitong.

Day 2: Ahaldara & Departure

Wake up early and drive to Ahaldara Viewpoint for a breathtaking sunrise over Kanchenjunga. After breakfast, visit Namthing Pokhari before heading back to NJP/Bagdogra for your onward journey.
